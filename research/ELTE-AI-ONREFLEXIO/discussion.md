## Summary: brief recap of the main findings

## Comparison: Connect the findings to the literature

## Implications: How does the world/theory change now that we know this?

## Direct Answer: Start by answering your main research question directly based on the evidence.

## The "Why": Offer explanations for why you think you got these results. If a hypothesis was rejected, don't bury it—explain why the reality differed from the theory.

## The "Comparison": This is the most important part. Revisit your Theoretical Background. If your results are the "new guest" at the party, how are they interacting with the "old guests" (previous authors)?

## The "Pivot": Acknowledge the "Risks". Be your own toughest critic before the peer reviewers do it for you.
